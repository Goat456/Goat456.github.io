Boeing B-25 Mitchell

The Boeing B-25 Mitchell is a medium bomber used during WW2. Its most notible mission was the Doolittle Raid, where it participated in the bombing of Japan. The B-25 was designed by North American Aviation, Inc., in response to a prewar requirement and was first flown in 1940. A high-wing monoplane with a double tail and tricycle landing gear, it is powered by two 1,700-horsepower Wright radial engines, had a wingspan of 67 feet 7 inches, was 53 feet 6 inches long, and carried a crew of four to six. The B-25 had a range of about 1,350 miles (depending on the weather and other variables), a maximum speed of about 300 miles (480 km) per hour, and a ceiling of 24,000 feet (7,300 metres). It could carry 3,000 pounds (1,360 kg) of bombs inside the plane in the bomb bay and more externally on underwing racks.
